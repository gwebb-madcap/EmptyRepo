Customer invoices provide a history of payments tendered for parts sold. You can create invoices while working with customers to find the parts they need or when acknowledging messages for online orders, orders from other sources, or returns.

Create an invoice

  1. From the Invoice screen, enter the customer number.

  1. If Authorized buyer is set up for the account, you must select one.

  1. To add parts, you can search the Catalog, or enter the Part number.

A bar code does not scan? Select Barcode to manually enter it.

  • Line Select the appropriate product line when the product is shared among multiple lines.

  • Enter the Quantity.

    I do not have sufficient on-hand quantities? 

    A message tells you the parts will be sourced in .

  • Price or PD Enter a new price or price decision code to override the price.

    Store managers must approve a price override.

  • Tax or Tax 2 Tax are automatically applied based on local sales tax.

  • Confirm part information is correct, and then enter Y for OK.

  1. Repeat until all parts are added.

  2. Check out and tender payment or go to Special functions, and then select Save to save the invoice and open it when payment is received.

Create an invoice from messages Invoice special orders

When you receive parts for special orders, open the saved invoice to prepare it for delivery or pick up.

 About price decision codes

Store managers must approve a price decision code.

Any user can enter a price decision code. Price decision codes can be set up to require a password.

Pricing decision codes let you adjust the price of a part when you need to change it from the suggested price. For example, you may want to match a competitor's price, or offer a customer the lowest price when a part label is incorrect. To do this, you can enter a price decision code from a pricing sheet.
