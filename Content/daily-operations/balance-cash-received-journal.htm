The final step in closing each business day is to reconcile all transactions for that day. As you do this, verify the funds collected match the transactions in , and then enter amounts or make corrections in the Cash received journal if you find discrepancies. We refer to this as balancing and finalizing the Cash received journal.

The Cash received journal is a record of the total funds your store collects for the business day and compares them to the transactions completed in . When funds collected match the funds from completed transactions, the Cash received journal is balanced and can be finalized. When these do not match, it is out of balance. You need to make changes to bring it into balance before you can finalize it.

  • Actual funds collected during checkout.

  • Payments received on accounts (ROA).

  • Miscellaneous expenses.

  • Overages and shortages.

Once in balance, you are ready to finalize the Cash received journal, and close your business day.

If you skip a day, you must balance and finalize the Cash received journal for the skipped day before you can balance the next day.

If you skip multiple days, balance them starting with the oldest date first.

To keep accounting records current, balance and finalize the Cash received journal at the end of each accounting day.

         What to do when your Cash received journal is out of balance

    If the Out of balance message displays after balancing the Cash received journal, bring it into balance using Over and Short only if you are certain of the reason for the overage or shortage.
