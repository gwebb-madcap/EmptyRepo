The state of store data impacts your bottom line. Maintaining healthy data ensures you are supporting your customers, employees, and meeting your business and sales goals. While the immediate impact may be small, it adds up over time. Outdated store data affects your daily operations and sales. For instance, outdated bin labels result in selling parts at incorrect and potentially low prices. To maintain accurate store data, we recommend store managers complete the tasks listed below everyday.

Completing these daily operations, keeps your store data healthy. The main benefits of being consistent with these daily operations are:

  • Keeps on-hand inventory current.

  • Removes defective and core parts from your inventory.

  • Balances customer accounts.

  • Makes sure you have the latest version.

  • Provides a daily back up store data so you can revert to a previous day should an emergency occur.

Store opening operations

Before store opening, complete the following tasks to update on-hand inventory for the day's sales:

  • Receive freight (manually or with an RF scanner) and post freight to update on-hand inventory with parts arriving in overnight shipments.

  • Receive and post interstore transfer orders from the previous day to update inventory with quantities that have already arrived.

  • Invoice special orders from overnight shipments so they are ready for pick up or delivery.

  • Verify any parts returned to your store the previous day are still at your store by checking them against the COM130 Special invoice recap report.

Store closing operations

After the store closes, complete the following tasks to finalize the day's transactions, update inventory, update system applications, backup the store data, and reconcile discrepancies from the day's sales.

  • If you purchased merchandise for your customers from a source outside the supply chain, like a local auto dealership or wholesaler, complete the Buyout purchase order process.

  • Start the End of day process to close the business day, back up your store data, and process the latest version of if one is available.

  • Reconcile and balance all transactions for the accounting day.
