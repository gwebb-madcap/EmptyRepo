The quickest way to receive parts from the overnight shipment or factory-direct orders, is using a check-in group to receive all the parts from all the purchase orders arriving in the shipment at a time. After receiving, continue to post inventory so your on-hand quantities are up-to-date.

To keep track of parts while receiving:

  • Arrange and label staging areas of floor space or empty totes, like sales area, stocking area, or by part type.

  • Separate parts as you enter received quantities to keep track of what you have received.

  • Separate parts you received that are not part of the check-in group to determine their correct purchase order.

  • Keep parts you have not entered received quantities for on or near the shipping pallet.

  • Place parts you have entered received quantities for in the corresponding floor space or tote.

Create a check-in group of all purchase orders from the overnight shipment before receiving parts.

 Receive freight from partial shipments

When the quantity of shipped parts is more than the quantity you received, it may due to the parts on the purchase order being partially shipped. This happens when factory direct orders ship from multiple locations and at different times.

Enter the quantity of parts you received and flag the parts with quantities you expect to receive later with the Partial ship flag. This keeps those purchase orders open so you can receive and update the quantities when parts arrive.

  1. From menu, go to Inventory > Purchase orders > Received goods > Edit and post.

  2. Select the purchase order to receive, and then select Edit and post by purchase order.

    The Received goods screen shows parts from the purchase order. The Quantity shipped and Quantity rec are different because all parts have not yet arrived.

  3. Update the Qty rec for parts that have arrived.

  4. To flag parts that have not arrived as Partial ship:

  1. Select Save, and then Post freight as normal.

  2. When the remaining shipment arrives, open the same purchase order. In Quantity rec, add the quantity that has arrived to the original quantity.
