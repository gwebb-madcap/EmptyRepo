Receipt adjustments without purchase orders

Use Receipts to add parts to your on-hand quantities when you receive parts outside the supply chain and do not have a purchase order, like orders you take on a phone call with a supplier or with a customer for a special order.

  1. From the menu, go to Inventory > Adjustments, and then select the Receipts tab.

  2. Enter the Packing slip number, Part number, and then select a product line in the Line dropdown menu.

    A part is on an open purchase order? A message lets you know there is an open purchase order with the part number. Post the purchase order before adjusting the quantities.

  3. Enter Adjustment quantities:

  • On hand Enter a positive or negative quantity for the adjustment. For example, if you have 3 parts on hand and receive 2 parts, enter 2.

  • On order Enter the quantity of the part currently on order.

  • On backorder Enter the quantity of the part on backorder.

  1. Enter the Cost and Core cost as needed.

  1. Receipt options Optional Automatically updates Adjustment quantity based on purchase orders.

  • All items were received Select if all parts listed on the receipt are in the shipment.

  • On order quantity received Select if the on-order quantity is being received in this adjustment. This automatically updates On hand adjustment with the current on-order quantity, reduces the on-order quantity to 0, and clears Days on order for this part.

  • Back order quantity received Select if the backorder was canceled. This occurs when the distribution center cancels a backorder and you do not cancel it. This reduces the On back order quantity to 0 clears the Days on back order.

  • Cancel on order Select if you canceled the order.

  • Cancel back order Select if the backorder was canceled. This occurs when the distribution center cancels the order and you do not cancel it. This reduces the On backorder quantity to 0 and clears Days on back order.

  • Cancel both order and back order Select to cancel both the order and back order.

  1. Select Save to update your on-hand quantities.

 Stock adjustments for damaged or lost parts

Use Stock adjustments to update on-hand quantities for damaged, lost, or found parts. You can also adjust core and defect quantities using Stock adjustments. For example, if your store donates oil filters, and you need to remove them from your inventory, enter negative quantities.

  1. From the menu, go to Inventory > Adjustments. The Receipts tab is open be default.

  2. Select the Stock adjustments tab.

  3. Enter the Part number, and then select a product line in the Line dropdown menu

  4. Enter Quantity adjustment:

  • On hand Enter a positive or negative quantity for the adjustment (not the total on-hand). For example, if shows 3 but you actually have 4, enter 4 as the adjustment.

  • Returned cores Enter a positive or negative quantity to correct the current Returned core quantity when a discrepancy is found. For example, if a customer leaves a core at your store's door overnight, you won't have an return transaction to increment the returned core quantity. In this case, make an adjustment. You may also find a discrepancy when working with the

  • Returned defectives Enter a positive quantity of the defective parts returned.

  1. Select Save to update your on-hand quantities.

  2. Select if you want to include adjustments in sales history:

  • Yes Updates your current month's sales quantity (a positive adjustment reduces the current month's sales quantity; a negative adjustment increases the current month's sales quantity).

  • No Does not update current month's sales quantity.

 Adjust for mixing paint

Use Mixing paint adjustments to remove opened mixing paint cans from inventory when customers request custom paint colors. Opened paint cans are non-returnable. You must remove them from your on-hand quantities.

  1. From the menu, go to Inventory > Adjustments, and then select the Mixing paint adjustments tab.

  2. Enter the Part number.

  3. Select the product line in the Line dropdown menu.

  4. To remove opened cans from inventory, enter a negative quantity in Quantity adjusted.

  5. Select Save to update your on-hand quantities.
