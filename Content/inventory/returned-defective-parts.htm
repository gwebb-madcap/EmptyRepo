When customers return defective parts, keep them in a unique staging area until you're ready to return them to the distribution center. Removing returned defective parts from your inventory is a multi-step process, that requires you to:

  • Use the to confirm parts were returned correctly, and part numbers returned are in the correct staging areas.

  • Create the to validate part numbers and quantities being returned. What is a better word than validate for this step?

  • Remove returned parts from your inventory.

  • Stage returned parts that can be resold away from the defective staging area and label New returns.

  • Complete all steps in the process before starting End of day.

  • Clear Use RGN to use Returned Defectives/Cores. Where is it? Go to System mgt > RGN controls.

  • Required to confirm returned defective part transactions match the total defective parts staged.

Process defective parts

  1. Build and generate the to confirm the total of returned defective parts matches the total in your staging area.

  • Go to Inventory > Returned defectives/cores. The Selection criteria opens.

  • Choose Select or use All to see all product lines to see all product lines with a returned defective quantity greater than 0.

  • In the Select product line screen, select all product lines from the Available list. Select the Right arrow to move to line(s) to the Selected list.

  • In the Selected list, select cores and other product lines with no returns, like BAT, and then select the Left arrow .

  • Repeat as needed to remove parts from the report.

  • Select OK.

  • Select Submit. Records returned shows the total of parts returned.

  • Select the Returned defectives/cores tab, and then select Returned defects to create the list of defective parts returned by product lines.

  • Select Printer . The Report viewer opens the report.

  • To confirm the Quantity for each core product line matches the quantities staged in your store, select Print to print the .

  1. After confirming part numbers and returned quantities are correct, you are ready to finalize and remove returned cores from returned core quantity:

  • Select Finalize cores.

  • Zero returned cores on-hand quantities for NAPA parts Required. Sets the returned defective quantity for the parts selected in the criteria to 0 (also called zeroes out).

  • Zero returned cores on hand quantities for non-NAPA parts Required. Sets the returned defective quantity for non- parts selected in the criteria to 0.

  1. Package defective parts for shipping.

    You can include a copy of the report as a packing slip.
