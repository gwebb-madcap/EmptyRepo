Stock adjustment, "Include adjustments in Sales history." Answer Yes, to update your current month's sales quantity (adjust by + no, sales history is reduced by that number; adjust by - no, sales history is increased for the current month totals). Answer No, to keep the sales quantity history as is. For example, if you adjust the quantity for an air filter from by 1 and answer Yes, last month's sales quantity

Cost/adjustment What it means

Replacement cost

Cost to replace the part from the distribution center.

Average cost

Average cost of part calculated as:

((On-hand quantity x Replacement cost) + (Receipt quantity x Receipt cost) ) / Total resulting quantity on-hand

Landed cost

Total cost to ship part to store, includes shipping fees.

Core cost

Cost your store pays for the core part. The system charges the customer for the core cost and refunds it when core is returned to the store.

Landed core cost

Total cost to ship core to store, includes shipping fees. Is this correct?

Current adjustment

Total quantities of on-hand, returned cores, and returned defective parts.

Quantity adjusted

Positive or negative number you enter to adjust inventory.

New quantity

Total of Current and Quantity adjusted.

Stock adjustment value

Cost of the adjustment. what does this really mean?

Landed value

Total value of the adjustment, includes shipping fees. Is this correct?

On hand

 

Quantity of part currently in stock at your store. Is this correct?

Returned cores

Returned defectives
