When customers return core and defective parts, you probably leave them in their staging areas until you're ready to send them on their way. However, to keep your inventory accurate, make sure to remove them from your on-hand quantities before shipping them.

Removing returned core and defective parts from your on-hand inventory is a multi-step process, that requires you to:

  • Confirm the parts are in your store using the .

  • Create the and to confirm the returned quantities match return transactions.

  • Remove returned parts from your on-hand inventory.
