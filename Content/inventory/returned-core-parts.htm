You probably keep returned core parts in a specific staging area until you're ready to ship them to the manufacturer. Before sending them on their way, remove them from inventory to keep inventory up-to-date. Removing returned core parts from inventory is a multi-step process:

  • Use the to confirm parts were returned correctly, and part numbers returned are in the correct staging areas.

  • Create the to validate part numbers and quantities being returned. What is a better word than validate for this step?

  • Remove returned parts from your inventory.

  • Stage returned parts that can be resold away from your core staging area and label New returns.

  • Complete all steps in the process before starting End of day.

Process returned cores

  1. Build and generate the to confirm the total of returned cores matches the total in your staging area.

  • Go to Inventory > Returned defectives/cores. On the Selection criteria tab, choose the product lines to include on the .

  • Enter the Product line code for all cores returned in the Line field (like, BAT), and then press Enter.

    I don't know the product line? Select All to see all product lines with a returned core quantity greater than 0 before selecting criteria.

  • Repeat until all core product lines being returned are selected.

  • Select Submit. Records returned shows the total of parts returned.

  • To create a list of core parts returned by product lines, select the Returned defectives/cores tab, and then select Returned cores.

  • Select Printer . The Report viewer opens the report.

  • To confirm the Quantity for each core product line matches the quantities staged in your store, select Print to print the .

  1. After confirming part numbers and returned quantities are correct, you are ready to finalize and remove returned cores from returned core quantity:

  • Select Finalize cores.

  • Zero returned cores on-hand quantities for NAPA parts Required. Sets the returned core quantity for the parts selected in the criteria to 0 (also called zeroes out).

  • Zero returned cores on hand quantities for non-NAPA parts Required. Sets the returned core quantity for non- parts selected in the criteria to 0.

  • Select OK to update remove returned core quantities from inventory.

  1. Stage returned cores (for distribution center) and non- cores (for vendor) on separate shipping pallets.

    You can include a copy of the printed report as the packing slip.
