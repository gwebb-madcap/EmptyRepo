After receiving freight, the next step is to add quantities to your on-hand inventory. As the store manager,

  • Confirm the received quantities are correct,

  • Make final adjustments for overages, shortages, or damaged parts,

  • Flag parts as Partial ship when needed

  • Post inventory which updates on-hand quantities with the received quantity.

Overnight orders from the distribution center ( daily orders) and factory direct orders (drop-ship orders) typically contain parts from multiple purchase orders. These orders are automatically assigned to one check-in group so you can quickly receive all parts at a time. The check-in group is the Shipment number on the COM150 Shipment advise report

Because there are different options for receiving and posting freight, use the below table to decide which method we recommend based on the type of order arrive at your store:

 Post freight from special or interstore transfer orders

When parts arrive for special orders, interstore orders, or midday orders, post the received quantities when they arrive using the transfer document even if you did not receive the stock receipt.

  1. From the menu, go to Inventory > Purchase orders > Received goods > Open purchase orders.

  1. Select the Order type column to sort by order type.

  2. Select the interstore purchase order.

  1. Enter Quantity rec.

    Use Receive all if Quanitty shipped matches Quantity rec.

  2. Select Post to inventory.

  3. Repeat for all interstore orders received the previous day.

 Post freight using the partial ship flag

When the quantity of shipped parts is more than the quantity you received, it may be due to parts on the purchase order being partially shipped. This happens when the distribution center ships parts at different times or factory direct orders ship from multiple locations and at different times.

Enter the quantity of parts you received and flag the parts with quantities you expect to receive later with the Partial ship flag. This keeps those purchase orders open so you can receive and update the quantities when they arrive.

  1. From the menu, go to Inventory > Purchase orders > Received goods > Edit and post.

  2. Select the purchase order to post, select Edit and post by purchase order.  

    The Received goods screen shows parts from the purchase order. The Quantity shipped and Quantity rec are different when parts are shipped but have not arrived at your store.
