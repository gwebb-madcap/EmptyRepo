Customer accounts

APAR creates customer accounts for you; however, you can add or make changes when needed.

Customer accounts store information you need to do business with your repeat customers. You determine how much or how little information to keep on file. Customer information is where you add and maintain customer accounts. There's lots of data you can collect for each customer, like

  • Email addresses for sending invoices and statements.

  • Billing method for customers who want a purchase order or charge account on file.

  • Custom pricing for customers you want to give discounts.

  • Credit limits, statement types, and accounts receivable terms for accurate invoicing.

  • Authorized buyers who can make purchases on the account without verification.

  • Email addresses for invoices and statements.

Customer account types

Before creating a new customer account, consider what data you want to keep on file so you can decide which type of customer account to create, like a commercial account or individual account. Two types of customer accounts you can create are:

  • Retail Typically used for individual customers who may not be business owners but buy parts regularly and may want a payment method on file. If you want historical sales data so you can offer them special pricing, you need to create a retail account.

  • Regular In some stores, Regular accounts are called Commercial accounts, AR account, Customer file, or Charge accounts. These accounts are large or small businesses who purchase regularly from your store. These customers usually keep a payment method on file. By creating a Regular account, you can track accounts receivable balances, historical sales data, send statements, set up core tracking, and offer special pricing.

  • Default customer account It's also common for stores to have a Default customer account (that is a Retail account type). This is a generic account not associated with a specific customer. Use it to create invoices for customers when you don't want to keep customer information on file. In some stores, Default customer account is also called Cash customer or Customer 0.

Store ownership groups

Maintain customer account information at the main store and send changes to your branch stores. If you are using Multi Store, branch stores automatically receive updates.

If you are not using Multi Store, you'll see the a message when you close the Customer information screen asking if you want to send changes to branch stores. Choose Yes to send updates now.
