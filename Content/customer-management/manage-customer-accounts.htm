 Buyout po (pickup) price information

The Pickup G/P information sets up gross profit percentage used to calculate the sale price for parts (for customers) or merchandise (for your store) you purchase outside of the supply chain. You can add multiple ranges to cover different price ranges. For example, a 50% gross profit for parts selling between $1.00-$99.00; and a 25% gross profit for parts selling between $100-$150.

  1. Select New to add a new pickup gross profit percent range.

  2. In Beginning cost enter the starting cost in the range.

  3. In Ending cost enter the ending cost in the range.

  4. In Gross profit % enter the percentage of gross profit to add to the price for that range.

  5. Select a Rounding type to determine if price is rounded.

Choose Yes to round according to the rounding you have set up. Where do I set up Rounding tables?  Go to System Mgt > Invoicing > Rounding tables.

Blanket purchase order

Set up purchase order numbers for the customer to charge against. Blanket purchase orders establish a charge limit for each invoice you create for this customer and does not limit the number of invoices for the customer. When you add a Blanket po, the purchase order is required when creating invoices. You can create blanket purchase orders for different date ranges, each with a unique maximum amount. For example, a customer may want to limit charges on each invoice to $100 during January. In this case, create a Blanket po beginning on January 1 and ending on January 31 with a Maximum amount of $100. Every invoice with total charges under $100 is charged against this purchase order.

  1. To create a Blanket po, select New .

  2. Complete the following required information:

  • PO number Required. Purchase order number used on the customer's invoices. Are there any restrictions or requirements for the number?

  • Begin date First date purchases can be made against this purchase order number.

  • End date Last date purchases can be made against this purchase order number.

  • Maximum amount Maximum dollar amount allowed on each invoice.

Sales summary

Gives a summary of the customer's sales information for comparing trends during different date ranges, like sales totals, like Net sales, Gross profit, Returns, and Invoice count. How do they use this information? 

Retake screen with data

Column

What it shows

Net sales

Total value of goods sold to the customer. (Sum of customer sales - Sum customer sales returns).

Net cost

Total cost of goods sold to the customer. (Sum of customer sales cost - Sum of customer sales returns cost).

Gross profit

Total amount of gross profit in dollars and the percentage of total sales. (Net Sales from selected period – Net Cost from selected period) (Percentage = Gross Profit from selected period / Net Sales from selected period) * 100).

Returns

Total value of goods returned by the customer.

Return % of sales

Percent of returns in comparison to sales. Returns % of Sales = (sum of Sales Returns) / (sum of Customer Sales) * 100.

Invoice count

Total number of invoices created for the customer.

Net core sales

Total value of Core sales.

Net core cost

Total cost of core sales.

Net reportable sales

If the customer participates in the manufacturers rebate program, the total value of reportable goods sold under the program.

Net reportable cost

Total cost of reportable goods sold. Rebate amounts are calculated as a percentage of this cost.

Reportable gross profit

Total amount of gross profit for reportable goods sold and the percentage of total reportable sales.

Net delivered sales

Total value of goods delivered to the customer.

Net delivered cost

Total cost of goods delivered to the customer.

Delivered gross profit

Total dollar amount of gross profit for delivered goods and percentage of total delivered sales.

Delivered invoice count

Number of invoices created for goods delivered to the customer.

Net access sales

Total value of goods purchased under the Access program.

Net access cost

Total cost of goods purchased under the Access program.

Access gross profit

Total amount of gross profit for goods purchased under the Access program and percentage of net Access sales.

Access invoice count

Number of invoices created for goods purchased using the Access program.

Defective returns

Total value of defective goods returned.

WLC returns

Total value of WLC (Warranty Labor Claims).

 Misc charges

Miscellaneous charges tab is where you assign charges outside the normal cost and exceptions to charges to the customer account. For example some states require a disposal fee on batteries. You can add this miscellaneous Wholesale customers selling batteries are not required to pay the fee. To make the customer account exempt from this fee, assign a miscellaneous exemption. When you sell this customer a battery, they will be exempt from the disposal fee charge.

Are there default charges I can list? 

  1. To assign a miscellaneous charge, select the charge from Available.

  2. Select the Up arrow. The Misc charge is Assigned.

  3. Select Use miscellaneous charge exemptions? to apply the charges in the Assigned list to the customer's account.

Required government certificates

If the customer has certifications, you can add them to the customer's account on the Government certificate tab. A common government certificate is the EPA refrigerant certification required to sell freon.

  1. To assign a government certificate, select the certificate from Available.

  2. Select the Up arrow.

  3. In the Certification information screen, enter the following required details:

  • Certification number 6-digit certificate number.

  • Technician name First and last name of the certified employee.

  • Effective and Expiration Date certification is valid.

  • Select Save.

    The certification is Assigned to the customer account.
