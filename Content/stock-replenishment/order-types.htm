Order types

Replenishment ordering is made easier when you are familiar with the order types that are available in . All order types except non-NAPA are transmitted automatically by .

Orders that you pick up locally and immediately resell are called buyout orders and are created using the Buyout PO option. The orders described below are created using the Create Order option on the Inventory menu.

Order type

Description

NAPA

NAPA parts ordered from your local NAPA distribution center (DC) on a daily basis.

considers parts on order when determining how many to order.

Non-NAPA

Parts ordered from outside the NAPA supply chain. You must print these purchase orders and deliver them to your vendor.

Factory

Parts ordered directly from the manufacturer to maximize discounts. These orders use FOTABs (factory order tables), which define what is included in a special program. Factory orders in are based on the terms and discounts manufacturers are currently offering.

If a FOTAB is missing, contact Support.

Special

Which definition is correct? Parts ordered for a customer at a specific store or an order from a local supplier, such as an auto dealer, for merchandise that you do not normally stock. This is from previous help and doesn't match what was discussed in demo. Demo indicates special orders are NAPA orders with special terms, such as pre-orders for a product with bulk discounts.

does not consider special orders when determining quantities for other others. For example, if you have a special order for a pallet of antifreeze to be delivered on a future date, the quantity in the special order is not considered when you place a regular NAPA order for antifreeze.
